1. Introduction

Algebra is a branch of mathematics dealing with symbols and rules for manipulating those symbols. It forms the foundation of higher mathematics and is essential in problem-solving.

2. Key Concepts

  • Variables: Symbols (like x, y) representing numbers.
  • Expressions: Combinations of variables and numbers (e.g., 2x + 5).
  • Equations: Statements showing two expressions are equal (e.g., 2x + 5 = 10).

4. Example Problems

Example 1: Solve for x in 2x + 5 = 15.

2x = 15 - 5
2x = 10
x = 10 / 2 = 5

Example 2: Find the slope of a line passing through (2,3) and (6,7).

m = (7 - 3) / (6 - 2) = 4 / 4 = 1

5. Root Concept

The roots of algebra trace back to **al-Khwarizmi**, a Persian mathematician whose book "Al-Kitab al-Mukhtasar fi Hisab al-Jabr wal-Muqabala" introduced algebra to the world.

6. Imitative Explanation

Think of algebra as a **puzzle** where you find missing numbers. If you have `2x + 5 = 15`, you're solving for `x` just like finding a missing piece in a jigsaw puzzle.
