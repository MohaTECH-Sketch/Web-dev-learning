1. Introduction

Motion is the change in position of an object over time. Forces are interactions that cause objects to accelerate or change direction.

2. Types of Motion

  • Linear Motion: Motion in a straight line (e.g., a car moving on a highway).
  • Circular Motion: Motion along a circular path (e.g., Earth orbiting the Sun).
  • Oscillatory Motion: Repetitive back-and-forth movement (e.g., a pendulum).

3. Formulas

Speed = Distance / Time
Velocity = Displacement / Time
Acceleration = (Final Velocity - Initial Velocity) / Time

4. Newton‚Äôs Laws of Motion

  • First Law (Inertia): An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.
  • Second Law (F = ma): The force applied to an object is equal to its mass times acceleration.
  • Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

5. Example Problems

Example 1: A car travels 150 meters in 5 seconds. What is its speed?

Speed = 150m / 5s = 30 m/s

Example 2: A force of 10N is applied to a 2kg object. What is its acceleration?

Acceleration = Force / Mass = 10N / 2kg = 5 m/s¬≤
